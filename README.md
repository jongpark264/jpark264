#I created a scatter plot to compare the average price and average minimum stay for each region by dividing Washington D.C. into five regions (East, West, South, North, and Central). Before I create a plot, I used 'filter' to remove long-term stay listings where minimum nights exceed 31 days which is not common. After filtering, more than 1,500 data points were filtered, and I found out through searching that applying this filter can significantly reduce observations due to Washington D.C.'s lodging regulations. In other words, places where a minimum stay of 30 days or more is set can be seen as having a high probability of being a boarding house or room sharing rather than a business establishment. A key insight observed in the plot is that the center of the city has the highest average price and at the same time occupies the largest number of listings with a relatively high price range.
